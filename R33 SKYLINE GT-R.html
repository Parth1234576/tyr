The Nissan Skyline GT-R R33 is the third generation of the GT-R model and was produced from 1995 to 1998. The R33 is larger and heavier than its predecessor, the R32, but also more refined and powerful.

The R33 GT-R was powered by a 2.6-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six engine that produced up to 280 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. It featured a 5-speed manual transmission and Nissan's advanced ATTESA all-wheel-drive system, which provided exceptional traction and handling.

The R33 GT-R also received several upgrades and improvements over the previous generation, including a stiffer chassis, larger brakes, and improved aerodynamics. It also featured a more advanced version of Nissan's HICAS four-wheel steering system, which provided better stability and handling at high speeds.

Despite being less popular than the R32 and R34 models, the R33 GT-R is still highly regarded by enthusiasts for its unique styling, advanced technology, and impressive performance. It has also gained a reputation as a capable and versatile platform for tuning and modifications, making it a popular choice among car enthusiasts and tuners around the world.
